Global temperature record shows 2016 as the warmest year on record

The graphic shows the global mean surface temperature change since 1880 till nowadays. The grey curve indicates the annual average temperature and the black curve shows the smoothed result. Over the last two centuries, the mean temperature has increased by around 1 degree Celsius.

The global temperature record shows the fluctuations of the temperature of the atmosphere and the oceans through various spans of time. The recently published data by NASA is one of the most detailed information that exists since 1850 when methodical thermometer-based records began.

Sixteen of the 17 warmest years in the 136-year record have all occurred since 2001, with the exception of 1998. The year 2016 ranks as the warmest on record.
